trustworthy multimodal AI for medicine — reliable diagnosis, robust under attack, and grounded in clinical knowledge.
My work centres on making vision-language models actually deployable for clinical use. In practice this requires three things at once: (i) the model must reason like a clinician, not pattern-match; (ii) it must hold up under adversarial or out-of-distribution inputs; and (iii) it should not depend on expensive labelled data for every new disease or hospital. Below is the research thread.

1. Multi-Expert Chain-of-Thought Reasoning for Medical Diagnosis
Single-pass VLMs are easy to fool and hard to audit. We build hierarchical-expert reasoning frameworks that produce intermediate diagnostic evidence, so the final answer is both more accurate and explainable. MedCoT (EMNLP 2024 Oral) introduces a sparse mixture-of-experts hierarchy; a 256M-parameter MedCoT outperforms LLaVA-Med (7B) by 5%+. Follow-ups include V2T-CoT (MICCAI 2025), IFCoT (IEEE TCSVT 2026, IF 11.1), and MedThink (NAACL Findings 2025).

2. Adversarial Robustness of Vision-Language Models
Imperceptible perturbations can flip CLIP's prediction confidently — in medicine this is a safety problem. SCC (ICML 2026) is a retraining-free, inference-time defence that fights back via two self-checks: a soft text anchor and multi-view aggregation. SCC restores robust accuracy by 12–16 percentage points over prior test-time defences across 22 benchmarks and 5+ attack families, with negligible clean-accuracy loss. It plugs into CLIP, OpenCLIP, and BioMedCLIP. [PDF] [Code]

3. Vision-Language Alignment for Zero-Shot Medical Diagnosis
Class-name proxies are too impoverished for medical concepts. We propose multimodal proxy mining: instead of using a single text embedding as the class representative, we learn richer proxies that bridge the vision-text modal gap. KPL (AAAI 2025 Oral) is fully training-free: it builds an LLM-generated knowledge-enhanced base, retrieves image-relevant descriptions, and uses Stable Greenkhorn to produce multimodal proxies. KPL improves zero-shot accuracy by up to +24.7 percentage points (avg) over vanilla CLIP across 5 medical datasets, and outperforms SoTA baselines such as InMaP and Hierarchical. VPL (EMNLP 2024 Findings) is the predecessor, recovering visual proxies via stable Sinkhorn iteration. [KPL PDF] [VPL PDF]

4. Brain-to-Visual Decoding
Extending the trustworthy-multimodal thread into neuroscience. MindAdapter (KDD 2026, AI4Sciences) is a parameter-efficient residual calibration module that adapts cross-subject brain-to-visual decoding models with only a few examples, addressing the chronic problem of per-subject distribution shift in fMRI/EEG decoding. [Project]

5. Clinically Deployed Multimodal Fusion
Earlier work that I am proud has actually reached the clinic. Our Cell Press Patterns paper presents the first clinically applicable 3D fusion pipeline for crown-root-bone reconstruction from CBCT + intraoral mesh scans, reducing reconstruction time from 5–7 hours to 20–25 minutes (~15× speed-up) and now deployed in the Angelalign IRS clinical system. [PDF] Related work on tooth segmentation (ToothSegNet, ISBI 2023; TSNet, MIDL 2023) underpins this pipeline.
